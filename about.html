ABOUT ME

Rie Drolet
Graphic designer.

Student at Algonquin College in Ottawa. Studying interactive media design. She studied interior and residential design in Tokyo, Japan, and worked for a long time at a company that provided comprehensive design. The company was proposing new business ventures including research, format development, graphic design, architecture, merchandising, consulting, and television production. She was the merchandising director, came up with concepts, thought about tenant leasing and foot traffic in urban development, and was in charge of everything related to the soft side, such as in-store development, products, distribution, display, uniform design, and menu development. After that, she started her own company independently and did a lot of freelance work. Some of them include skin care, cosmetics packaging director, supplement development, architectural symposium coordinator, public art coordinator, overseas report coordinator for television shows and movies, and graphic designer. In 2018, she moved to Ottawa, Canada, and worked as a graphic designer and Japanese translator. In graphic design, she designs magazines and business cards. She designs logos, signs, and banners. ・Illustrator ・Photoshop ・InDesign She has no problem using them. Currently, she is focusing on digital design and works on a wide range of designs, including ・Web design ・Web development ・UX design ・Photography and video (Premier Pro)
